## Business Problem Statement

Candidates preparing for the Microsoft AI-901 certification exam who use general-purpose AI learning tools cannot ensure their study content is aligned with the official syllabus in terms of scope, knowledge structure, terminology, and depth. Candidates must spend additional time cross-validating AI responses against official materials, resulting in low learning efficiency and lack of confidence in study quality.

This problem negatively impacts the certification preparation experience and expected pass rates, while also limiting the value of AI tools in the certification training market.

## Impact Scope

| Dimension | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Learning Efficiency | Candidates perform double work — switching between AI and official sources for verification |
| Learning Confidence | Uncertainty about whether studied content matches exam requirements causes anxiety |
| Knowledge Retention | High verification cost leads to skipping validation when concepts feel fuzzy, leaving gaps |
| Exam Readiness | Systematic deviation from syllabus could negatively impact expected pass rates |

## Data Source

> Disclaimer: This is a BA learning project. Large-scale user research has not been conducted. The problem is based on the project initiator's first-hand certification preparation experience.

## Out of Scope

- This statement does not assume any specific technical solution
- This statement does not imply general-purpose AI tools are "not useful" — only that they have limitations in the certification preparation scenario
