## Methodology

- Personas are based on the project initiator's first-hand experience and secondary research
- No formal user interviews were conducted (scope limitation of a BA learning project)
- Personas are designed to be **distinct enough** that each drives different design decisions

---

## Persona A: The Fresh Graduate

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Name** | Eric Chan |
| **Age** | 22 |
| **Occupation** | Recent IT graduate |
| **Education** | BSc in Internet Technology / Computer Science |
| **Location** | Hong Kong |
| **Status** | Actively job hunting |

### Background
Eric recently graduated with an IT degree. He noticed many entry-level jobs in Hong Kong now prefer candidates with cloud/AI certifications. He decided to get the AI-901 certification to stand out from other fresh graduates.

### Goals
- Pass AI-901 exam on the first attempt
- Build a competitive edge in the job market
- Complete certification within 2-3 months

### Pain Points
- University coursework did not cover Microsoft-specific AI services
- Has time but doesn't know where to start
- Easily overwhelmed by the volume of official documentation
- Finds it hard to track what he has learned and what remains

### Frustrations
- "AI tools like ChatGPT answer well but I can't tell if it's teaching me the right stuff for the exam"
- "Microsoft Learn has everything but it's scattered — I keep losing track of where I was"

### Expectations
- A structured learning path that follows the exam syllabus
- Clear progress tracking
- Affordable or free options

### Device Usage
- Laptop (primary), Smartphone (secondary)

### Quote
> "I need to know exactly what to study and whether I'm ready for the exam."

## Persona B: The In-office IT Professional

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Name** | Sarah Wong |
| **Age** | 29 |
| **Occupation** | IT Support Analyst |
| **Education** | Higher Diploma in IT |
| **Location** | Hong Kong |
| **Status** | Full-time employed |

### Background
Sarah has been working in IT support for 4 years. Her company encourages upskilling and partially reimburses certification fees. She wants to transition from support to a cloud/AI role. She has limited study time between work and personal life.

### Goals
- Get certified without sacrificing work performance
- Study efficiently — only learn what's required for the exam
- Complete preparation in 4-6 weeks

### Pain Points
- Has only 30-60 minutes per day for study
- Cannot afford to waste time on out-of-scope content
- Needs to quickly verify specific concepts when questions arise during work
- Forgetfulness — often needs to revisit topics she studied weeks ago

### Frustrations
- "I don't have time to cross-check AI answers against official docs every time"
- "When I'm stuck on a concept at work, I need the answer NOW, not after 15 minutes of searching"

### Expectations
- Fast, precise answers focused on exam scope
- Session continuity — remembers where she left off
- Mobile-friendly for study during commute
- Willing to pay HK$200-500 for a tool that saves her time

### Device Usage
- Smartphone (commute), Laptop (evening study)

### Quote
> "Time is my scarcest resource. Help me study smarter, not harder."

## Persona C: The Career Switcher

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Name** | Michael Cheung |
| **Age** | 32 |
| **Occupation** | Marketing Executive (transitioning to AI/Tech) |
| **Education** | BBA in Marketing |
| **Location** | Hong Kong |
| **Status** | Self-studying while working full-time |

### Background
Michael has 8 years in marketing but sees AI transforming the industry. He decided to earn the AI-901 certification as his first step into the tech field. He has no formal IT or programming background. He finds many study materials assume prior technical knowledge.

### Goals
- Build foundational AI knowledge from zero
- Pass AI-901 as a stepping stone to more advanced certifications
- Understand concepts deeply enough to discuss them in job interviews

### Pain Points
- Official Microsoft documentation assumes technical familiarity
- AI tools use jargon he doesn't understand
- Does not know what "prerequisite knowledge" he's missing
- Anxious about whether self-study is sufficient without a structured course

### Frustrations
- "I asked ChatGPT to explain 'Azure AI services' and it used terms I'd never heard of"
- "Everyone says 'just read the docs' but the docs weren't written for beginners like me"

### Expectations
- Prerequisite explanations for technical concepts
- Plain-language explanations when needed
- Confidence tracking — "Am I actually learning or just reading?"
- Progress visible in a way that motivates continued effort

### Device Usage
- Laptop (primary)

### Quote
> "I'm not afraid of hard work. I'm afraid of working hard on the wrong things."

## Persona Comparison Matrix

| Dimension | Eric (Graduate) | Sarah (In-office IT) | Michael (Career Switcher) |
|-----------|----------------|---------------------|--------------------------|
| Technical background | IT degree | IT experience | Non-IT |
| Study time | 1-3 hrs/day | 30-60 min/day | 1-2 hrs/day |
| Budget | Low ($0-300 HKD) | Medium (reimbursed) | Low-Medium |
| Key concern | Know where to start | Time efficiency | Prerequisite gaps |
| Retention need | Medium | High (forgetful) | High (new concepts) |
| Mobile priority | Medium | High | Low |

## Assumptions

| # | Assumption |
|---|------------|
| 1 | Personas are based on the project initiator's observations, not formal market research |
| 2 | Hong Kong certification candidates share similar behavior patterns with global candidates |
| 3 | Three personas are sufficient to cover the main user segments for an MVP |
